## Product Practice References

Situation-indexed. Each practice names the authors who define the standard —
start here to know what good looks like.

Foundational source across all practices:
**PM Skills** — [phuryn](https://github.com/phuryn/pm-skills) — Structured PM

### When you need to understand customers and markets

The standard: continuous, evidence-based contact with real customers. Research
that produces decisions, not decks.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Teresa Torres** | *Continuous Discovery Habits* | Weekly discovery loops. Opportunity solution trees connecting outcomes to solutions. Decision guardrails grounded in evidence. |
| **Marty Cagan** | *INSPIRED; EMPOWERED* | Direct customer access for product teams. The difference between customer-informed and customer-driven. |
| **Erika Hall** | *Just Enough Research* | Practical, ethical research. Right method for the question. Avoids research theater. |
| **Rob Fitzpatrick** | *The Mom Test* | Questions about their life, not your idea. Prevents false positives in discovery interviews. |
| **Steve Blank** | *Four Steps to the Epiphany* | Customer development as hypothesis testing. Get out of the building before building. |
| **Tomer Sharon** | *Validating Product Ideas* | Lightweight validation patterns. Right fidelity for the stage of understanding. |
| **Steve Portigal** | *Interviewing Users* | Technique depth for qualitative work. Listening as a craft, not a checklist. |

### When you need to segment and define personas

The standard: segments that drive different product decisions, not marketing
demographics dressed up as personas.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Lenny Rachitsky** | *Essays/Podcast* | Broad practitioner patterns for segmentation. Practical over theoretical. |
| **Teresa Torres** | *Continuous Discovery Habits* | Segment by opportunity, not demographics. Personas as living hypotheses. |
| **Erika Hall** | *Just Enough Research* | Research-backed segments. Avoids persona fiction. |
| **Jim Kalbach** | *Mapping Experiences* | Ties segments to journeys and service touchpoints. |

### When you need to set vision and strategy

The standard: strategy as diagnosis + guiding policy + coherent action. Vision
that constrains choices, not a poster on the wall.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Marty Cagan** | *INSPIRED; EMPOWERED* | Product vision as a north star that empowers teams. Company-level vision vs. product vision vs. team mission. |
| **Richard Rumelt** | *Good Strategy Bad Strategy* | Strategy as diagnosis, guiding policy, and coherent action. Most "strategies" are just goals. |
| **Hamilton Helmer** | *7 Powers* | Strategic advantage patterns. Power dynamics that determine whether a strategy can sustain. |
| **Ben Thompson** | *Stratechery Essays* | Industry structure and aggregation theory. Context for strategy in platform economics. |

### When you need to understand product economics

The standard: economic thinking that connects product decisions to business
outcomes — not just revenue projections, but the physics of how value flows.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Melissa Perri** | *Escaping the Build Trap* | Outcomes over output. Product-led organizations. The economics of product management vs. project management. |
| **Don Reinertsen** | *Principles of Product Development Flow* | Cost of Delay, queues, batch size. The decision math underneath product economics. |
| **Alistair Croll & Ben Yoskovitz** | *Lean Analytics* | Stage-based metrics. One Metric That Matters. Pragmatic analytics for product economics. |
| **Brian Balfour** | *Essays/Models* | Growth and retention economics. System dynamics of growth loops. |

### When you need to sequence value and manage trade-offs

The standard: sequencing based on economic logic, not stakeholder volume.
Trade-offs made explicit, not hidden.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Don Reinertsen** | *Principles of Product Development Flow* | Cost of Delay as sequencing currency. WIP limits. Queue management. The physics of product development. |
| **John Cutler** | *Essays/Models* | Making trade-offs explicit. Systems thinking about why sequencing fails. Beautiful Systems as a diagnostic lens. |
| **Melissa Perri** | *Escaping the Build Trap* | Outcome-focused roadmaps. Escaping the trap of sequencing by loudest voice. |

### When you need to set guardrails and realize value

The standard: outcome framing that connects discovery to delivery. Guardrails
that prevent scope drift without preventing learning.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Teresa Torres** | *Continuous Discovery Habits* | Assumption mapping. Decision guardrails grounded in evidence. Connecting discovery to delivery. |
| **Jeff Gothelf** | *Lean UX; Sense & Respond* | Outcome framing + hypothesis-led delivery. Lean UX as a guardrail discipline. |
| **Don Reinertsen** | *Principles of Product Development Flow* | Economic guardrails. Batch size constraints as value realization levers. |

### When you need to manage flow and delivery

The standard: predictable delivery through flow management, not through
tighter deadlines or more status meetings.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Daniel Vacanti** | *Actionable Agile Metrics* | Lead time, WIP, throughput. Practical flow control. Predictability through data, not estimation theater. |
| **Don Reinertsen** | *Principles of Product Development Flow* | Queue management. Batch size reduction. Economic framing for flow decisions. |
| **Mary & Tom Poppendieck** | *Lean Software Development* | Lean principles applied to delivery. Eliminate waste, build quality in, deliver fast. |
| **John Cutler** | *Essays/Models* | Diagnosing why flow breaks. The organizational dynamics that create delivery dysfunction. |
| **Nicole Forsgren & Jez Humble** | *Accelerate* | DORA metrics. Evidence linking delivery practices to outcomes. Continuous delivery as a capability, not a tool. |

### When you need to manage risk and governance

The standard: risk controls embedded in the way of working, not bolted on as
gates that slow everything down.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Melissa Perri** | *Escaping the Build Trap* | Product ops maturity. Governance that enables rather than constrains. |
| **Nicole Forsgren & Jez Humble** | *Accelerate* | Change risk controls in the way of working. Evidence-based governance. |
| **Ann Cavoukian** | *Privacy by Design* | Risk posture as a design principle. Privacy and governance baked in, not inspected in. |
| **Gojko Adzic** | *Specification by Example; Impact Mapping* | Tightens validation and scope through examples. Specification as a risk management tool. |

### When you need to experiment and validate

The standard: experiments that produce real learning, not experiments as
theater to justify decisions already made.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Eric Ries** | *The Lean Startup* | Build-Measure-Learn. Cultural grounding for experimentation. MVP as a learning vehicle. |
| **Jeff Gothelf** | *Lean UX; Sense & Respond* | Hypothesis-led delivery. Experiments tied to outcomes, not features. |
| **Gojko Adzic** | *Specification by Example; Impact Mapping* | Impact mapping as experiment design. Concrete examples that make assumptions testable. |

## Systems and Organization Design

These aren't in the product practices canon but are foundational to how Jay
thinks about teams, alignment, and systems.

| Author | Anchor Work | What Defines Good |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais** | *Team Topologies* | Stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated subsystem team types. Cognitive load as a design constraint. Interaction modes. The foundational model for team structure. |
| **Donella Meadows** | *Thinking in Systems* | Stocks and flows, feedback loops, leverage points. The mental model underneath everything else. |
| **Stephen Bowers** | *The Art of Action* | Directed opportunism. Alignment through intent. Bridging the gaps between plans, actions, and outcomes. |
| **Will Larson** | *An Elegant Puzzle* | Engineering management as systems design. Fix teams before fixing backlogs. |
