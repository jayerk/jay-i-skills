# References: Influences on My Thinking

## Books and Frameworks

### Product Management

| Source | Key Ideas I Use |
|--------|----------------|
| **Continuous Discovery Habits** — Teresa Torres | Opportunity solution trees, weekly customer touchpoints, assumption mapping, experiment design. The backbone of how I think about discovery. |
| **Inspired / Empowered** — Marty Cagan | Empowered teams vs. feature factories, product leadership as context-setting, the difference between delivery teams and product teams. |
| **Escaping the Build Trap** — Melissa Perri | Product-led organizations, outcome-focused roadmaps, the difference between product management and project management. |
| **The Mom Test** — Rob Fitzpatrick | How to talk to customers without leading them. Every question should be about their life, not your idea. |

### Organizational Design

| Source | Key Ideas I Use |
|--------|----------------|
| **Team Topologies** — Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais | Stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated subsystem team types. Interaction modes. Cognitive load as a design constraint. The foundational model for how I think about team structure. |
| **The Principles of Product Development Flow** — Donald Reinertsen | Batch size, cost of delay, WIP limits, queue management. The physics of product development. |
| **An Elegant Puzzle** — Will Larson | Engineering management, organizational design, system thinking about teams. How to fix teams before fixing backlogs. |

### Strategy and Thinking

| Source | Key Ideas I Use |
|--------|----------------|
| **Good Strategy Bad Strategy** — Richard Rumelt | Strategy as diagnosis + guiding policy + coherent action. Most "strategies" are just goals. Real strategy identifies the crux. |
| **Thinking in Systems** — Donella Meadows | Stocks and flows, feedback loops, leverage points. The mental model underneath everything else. |
| **The Art of Action** — Stephen Bowers | Directed opportunism, alignment through intent, bridging the gaps between plans and outcomes. |

## AI-Native Workflow Systems

| Source | What I Take From It |
|--------|-------------------|
| **GSD (Get Shit Done)** — gsd-build | Context engineering as architecture. The STATE/ROADMAP/REQUIREMENTS pattern for persistent AI context. Phased planning with discuss→plan→execute→verify cycles. Multi-agent orchestration. Atomic commits per task. |
| **Superpowers** — obra | SKILL.md as a loadable work mode. YAML frontmatter for skill metadata. The brainstorm→spec→plan→execute→review discipline. Skills as mandatory workflows, not optional suggestions. Quality gates between phases. |
| **PM Skills** — phuryn | Structured PM frameworks as AI skills. The plugin/skill/command hierarchy. Discovery→strategy→execution taxonomy. Encoding proven PM frameworks into Claude's workflow for structure over generic output. |

## People I Learn From

| **Melissa Perri** | Product Strategy | Named the build trap and provided the escape route. Outcome-focused thinking over output-focused thinking. |
| **Jesse Fewell** | Agile Leadership | Community-minded approach to agile coaching. PMI engagement and practical frameworks for real teams. |
| **John Cutler** | Product Thinking | Relentless focus on the dysfunctions in how organizations do product work. Beautiful Systems is a lens I use regularly. |
