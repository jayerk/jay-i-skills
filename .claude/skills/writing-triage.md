# writing-triage

Post-generation quality gate — scans output for AI writing signals and
corrects before delivery. Cross-context: used by all output-producing skills.

TRIGGER when: any skill that produces written output reaches its final
review step. This skill runs automatically as a self-check before delivery.

DO NOT TRIGGER when: the output is code, data tables, or structured
artifacts where prose quality is not relevant (e.g., internal-tooling builds).

---

## Self-Check Mode

When referenced by another skill's quality check step, run the detection
categories below against the output you just generated. For every signal
detected, **rewrite to correct it before delivering**. Do not report the
triage results to the user — just fix the output silently.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is output that reads like it was
written by an experienced practitioner, not generated by a machine.

---

## Detection Categories

Check for signals in three areas:

1. Linguistic patterns
2. Factual plausibility
3. Stylistic consistency

---

## 1. Linguistic Pattern Signals

AI systems often produce balanced, polished sentence structures with
limited natural variation.

### Structural repetition

Look for:

* Repeated clause templates
* Similar sentence lengths across consecutive paragraphs
* Recurring rhetorical constructions (e.g., "While X provides benefits, Y also introduces challenges")

If the same structure appears repeatedly, rewrite with varied syntax.

### Vague generalities

Flag and replace filler phrases:

* "in today's world"
* "it is important to note"
* "in conclusion"
* "various factors"
* "a number of considerations"

Replace with concrete, specific language grounded in the domain.

### Unnatural transitions

Look for:

* Abrupt paragraph shifts
* Connective phrases without clear logic
* Summary statements that introduce new ideas rather than synthesize

### Voice uniformity

Flag and correct:

* Excessive hedging ("may," "might," "could potentially") — take a position
* Avoidance of firm statements — be direct
* Lack of personal perspective — ground in practitioner experience
* Absence of specific examples — add concrete detail

---

## 2. Fact and Source Verification

Large language models can produce confident but incorrect information.

### Statistics

Verify:

* The source organization exists
* The year is plausible
* The measurement unit makes sense

If a statistic cannot be verified, remove it or flag it for Jay to confirm.

### Quotes

Ensure:

* The quote appears in a verifiable source
* The wording matches the primary document
* The attribution is correct

### Research studies

Verify:

* The journal or publisher exists
* The study title is plausible
* DOI or publication page can be confirmed

Never invent citations. If uncertain, say so explicitly.

---

## 3. Tone and Style Consistency

Human writing reflects personal habits and lived experience. AI writing
drifts stylistically.

### Voice drift

Look for:

* Sudden shifts between formal and conversational tone
* Switching between British and American spelling
* Inconsistent punctuation or date formats

### Passive voice overuse

Flag patterns like:

* "The study was conducted..."
* "The results were analyzed..."
* "The findings were observed..."

Rewrite in active voice where it improves clarity.

### Missing experiential detail

If the content claims practitioner experience but lacks:

* Specific contexts or scenarios
* Concrete anecdotes
* Domain-specific friction points

Add grounding detail or remove the claim.

---

## Comparison Reference

| Trait | Practitioner Writing | AI-Generated Writing |
|-------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Sentence variety | Diverse structures and length | Uniform medium-length sentences |
| Personal perspective | Anecdotes and specifics | Generic observations |
| Error pattern | Minor natural variation | Grammatically perfect but shallow |
| Creativity | Novel framing, domain metaphors | Template phrasing |
| Depth | Nuanced argument with trade-offs | Broad but surface-level |

---

## The Deeper Signal

The real tell for machine writing isn't grammar or polish — humans can write
clean prose too. The deeper signal is **absence of lived friction**: no
sensory detail, no uneven edges, no stubborn specifics. Real experience
leaves fingerprints. Machines tend to wipe the surface smooth.

---

## External Evaluation Mode

This skill can also be used to evaluate writing of uncertain origin.

When evaluating external text, produce a structured assessment:

**AI Writing Triage Review**

* **Overall risk signal:** Low / Moderate / High
* **Detected indicators:** [list from categories above]
* **Evidence examples:** [short excerpts]
* **Recommended action:** verify citations / request additional detail / compare with known writing samples

**Important limitation:** This skill cannot conclusively determine
authorship. It identifies signals that justify further verification.
Human review and corroborating evidence are required for any final
determination.
