# Branding: PMI Buffalo Chapter

Visual and formatting standards for all PMI Buffalo chapter deliverables.
Read this alongside `_persona.md` — persona governs voice and tone, branding
governs visual presentation.

---

## When to Apply

Apply these standards when producing any artifact for PMI Buffalo chapter
operations: board meeting minutes, quarterly reports, annual chapter report,
member newsletters, event announcements, volunteer materials, strategic
benchmarks. If the output represents the chapter to members, PMI Global,
sponsors, or the WNY community, it follows these rules.

---

## Chapter Identity

- **Full name:** PMI Buffalo — Project Management Institute, Western New York Chapter
- **Short form:** PMI Buffalo (not "PMIWNY" or "PMI WNY Chapter")
- **Context:** Volunteer-run professional chapter serving the Western New York
  project management community

When the full name is needed (annual report header, formal correspondence),
use the full form. For all other artifacts, "PMI Buffalo" is sufficient.

---

## Header Block

Chapter documents use a simpler header than work templates — no framework
cross-references, no Produces/Consumes lines. Board members and volunteers
don't need the system plumbing.

```markdown
# [Type]: [Name]

> **PMI Buffalo** · [Type]
> Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
> Author: [Name / Role]
> Status: [Context-appropriate values]
```

- **PMI Buffalo** replaces "jay-i-skills template" — chapter artifacts identify
  with the chapter, not the skills system.
- **Author** includes role when relevant: `Jay VanDenBerg, VP Technology`
- **Date** uses ISO 8601 (`YYYY-MM-DD`), same as work context.

## Tables

| Rule | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Alignment | Left-align text. Center-align dates, counts, and status. |
| Headers | Sentence case. |
| Empty cells | Use `—` (em dash). |
| Status markers | Words: `Complete`, `In Progress`, `Pending`, `Deferred`. |
| Attendance | `Present`, `Absent`, `Excused` — for board meeting minutes. |

## Board Meeting Minutes Format

Minutes are the most frequent chapter artifact. Standard structure:

```markdown
# Board Meeting Minutes: [Month Year]

> **PMI Buffalo** · Meeting Minutes
> Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
> Location: [Venue / Virtual]
> Called to order: [Time]
> Adjourned: [Time]

## Attendance

| Name | Role | Status |
|------|------|:------:|
| [Name] | [Title] | Present |

## Agenda Items

### [Topic]
- **Presenter:** [Name]
- **Discussion:** [Summary]
- **Decision:** [What was decided]
- **Action:** [Who does what by when]

## Action Items

| # | Action | Owner | Due | Status |
|---|--------|-------|-----|:------:|
| 1 | [What] | [Who] | [Date] | Pending |
```

## Report Format

Quarterly and annual reports to PMI Global or the board:

- Lead with **membership numbers** and **PDU activity** — PMI Global cares
  about these first
- Include **financial summary** (revenue, expenses, balance) — board
  fiduciary duty
- **Event summary** with attendance counts — demonstrates chapter vitality
- **Volunteer recognition** — name volunteers who contributed, by name
- End with **next quarter priorities** — forward-looking, not just retrospective

---

## Formatting Conventions

Same base rules as work context, with chapter-specific adjustments:

- **Shorter documents preferred.** Volunteers scan, not study. Keep under 2 pages
  when possible.
- **Action items always have owners and dates.** Volunteer work drifts without
  named accountability.
- **PDU references include category.** "1.5 PDUs (Leadership)" not just "1.5 PDUs."
- **Sponsor mentions use full company name** on first reference, then short form.
- **Local references are appropriate.** WNY businesses, universities (UB, Canisius),
  local venues — this is a regional chapter.

---

## Tone in Artifacts

Per `_persona.md`, PMI Buffalo deliverables are professional but approachable,
volunteer-aware, and PMI-literate:

- **Board documents:** Crisp, structured, decision-focused. Respect volunteer time.
- **Member-facing:** Warm, inviting, community-minded. Encourage participation.
- **PMI Global submissions:** Formal, data-driven, aligned to global reporting
  requirements.
- **Sponsor communications:** Professional, gratitude-forward, value-oriented.

---

## What This Branding Is NOT

- **Not corporate enterprise branding.** This is a volunteer chapter, not a Fortune 500.
- **Not generic PMI branding.** Outputs should feel like they come from Buffalo
  specifically — not a template any chapter could use unchanged.
- **Not the work context.** No stakeholder navigation language, no portfolio
  management framing, no executive storytelling patterns. Chapter business
  is chapter business.
